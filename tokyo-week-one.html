The plan: a big home base in Tokyo, then the bullet train south to Kyoto and Osaka. This deck covers the first seven days — the Tokyo half.

Tokyo→Hakone→Tokyo→Kyoto & Osaka
Day1

Land & Ease In

Shinjuku by night

Arrival day, so the only goal is staying awake until a normal bedtime to beat the jet lag. Land in the afternoon, drop the bags, then wander Shinjuku as the neon comes on.

  • •Soak in the neon and crowds of Shinjuku↗
  • •Duck into Omoide Yokocho — tiny smoky food alley↗
  • •First yakitori skewers and your first real bowl of ramen↗
Keep it light · then crash
Day2

Old Tokyo Meets New

Asakusa + Skytree

A temple from the 600s in the morning, a futuristic tower in the afternoon — old and new Tokyo in a single day.

  • •Morning at Senso-ji, the city's oldest temple, in through the big red gate↗
  • •Snack the stall street — rice crackers, sweet bean cakes↗
  • •Tokyo Skytree — 634m, tallest tower in the world, glass-floor deck at ~450m with a Mt. Fuji view on a clear day↗
Tempura or sushi dinner nearby
Day3

The Tokyo From the Movies

Shibuya + Harajuku

The young, loud, high-energy side of the city — from a quiet forest shrine straight into the chaos.

  • •Start calm at the Meiji Shrine, a forest shrine in the middle of the city↗
  • •Harajuku & Takeshita Street — wild fashion, crepe stands, candy shops↗
  • •Shibuya Crossing where a thousand people cross at once + the Hachiko dog statue↗
Dinner & nightlife in Shibuya
Day4

Arcade Heaven

Akihabara + Ueno

The one Felix will go crazy for. Whole buildings that are nothing but arcades, seven or eight floors each.

  • •Akihabara — claw machines, fighting games, rhythm games, floors of it↗
  • •Super Potato — retro-game paradise, old Nintendo & Sega you can't find anywhere↗
  • •Anime, manga, figure shops + electronics stacked to the ceiling↗
  • •Afternoon flip to Ueno — big park, world-class museums↗
Evening: themed cafe, or street eats in Ameyoko alley
Day5

The Reset — Onsen Overnight

Hakone, in the mountains

The total opposite of the go-go-go city days. Ride the scenic Romancecar out from Shinjuku (~90 min) and slow all the way down.

  • •Mountain railway, a cable car over a steaming volcanic valley, and a pirate-ship cruise across the lake with Mt. Fuji behind it↗
  • •Check into a traditional ryokan inn↗
  • •Soak in the hot spring baths, throw on the robe, big multi-course dinner served in-room↗
  • •Sleep on futons on tatami · traditional breakfast + one more soak in the morning↗
Stay the night · the full slow-down
Day6

Back to the City

teamLab + Ginza

Romancecar back around midday, so keep it a clean two-part day — one big afternoon, one nice evening. No running around.

  • •teamLab — immersive digital art museum, rooms of light, mirrors and projections↗
  • •Ginza in the evening — Tokyo's upscale district, glossy stores and great restaurants↗
  • •Sit down for a really good dinner to cap the day↗
Easy after a travel morning
Day7

Flex Day

Your call

A feature, not filler. By now you'll both have a favorite — go back to whatever you loved most, or just take a slow morning.

  • •More arcades, more shopping, or a neighborhood to explore deeper↗
  • •Natural buffer before packing up for the bullet train to Kyoto↗
  • •Treat the evening as your last big Tokyo dinner↗
Rest · repeat a favorite · pack
