Day 8

Gion

The geisha district

Kyoto's most famous old quarter: dark wooden teahouses, paper lanterns, and stone lanes that look exactly like the Japan in your head. At dusk it turns cinematic, and if you're lucky a geiko or maiko slips past on her way to an appointment.

Things to do

01Hanamikoji Street

The postcard lane of preserved teahouses, best in the early evening light.

02Shirakawa Canal

A quieter, willow-lined stretch along the water, arguably prettier than the main drag.

03Yasaka Shrine

The bright vermilion shrine at Gion's end, its lantern-covered stage glows after dark.

04Pontocho Alley

Across the river, a narrow lane of restaurants stacked over the water.

05Kaiseki or kushikatsu

Dinner in the district can be a splurge or simple skewers, both done well.

Good to know

  • Dusk is the magic hour, time dinner around it.
  • Don't photograph geiko up close, side streets have fines posted.
  • Yasaka Shrine is free and open late, great after-dinner walk.
