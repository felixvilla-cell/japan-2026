明治神宮
Day 3

Meiji Shrine

A forest in the city

A Shinto shrine to Emperor Meiji, wrapped in a 170-acre planted forest right beside the chaos of Harajuku. You walk under towering wooden gates on gravel paths and the city noise just falls away.

Things to do

01The great torii

Twelve-meter cypress gates mark the entrance, among the largest wooden torii in Japan.

02Sake barrel wall

Rows of donated sake barrels (and a matching wall of French wine) line the approach.

03Main shrine

The quiet central courtyard, where you can write a wish on a wooden ema plaque.

04Inner Garden

A serene paid garden with an iris field and a spring, worth it in season.

05A wedding procession

Weekends often bring a traditional Shinto wedding walking through, all in white.

Good to know

  • It's free and opens with the sunrise.
  • Enter straight from Harajuku station.
  • Go early for the quietest, most magical version.
