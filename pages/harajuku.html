Day 3

Harajuku

Tokyo's fashion playground

The youth-fashion capital of Japan, where the candy-colored chaos of Takeshita Street sits a block from the designer boulevards of Omotesando. Loud, sweet, and endlessly people-watchable.

Things to do

01Takeshita Street

A pedestrian lane crammed with wild fashion, boutiques, and teenage energy.

02Rainbow treats

Foot-long cotton candy and overstuffed crepes, the street's signature snacks.

03Omotesando

The elegant tree-lined avenue, called Tokyo's Champs-Elysees, with striking flagship architecture.

04Cat Street

A relaxed backstreet of vintage shops and cafes connecting Harajuku to Shibuya.

Good to know

  • Takeshita is busiest and best on a weekend afternoon.
  • Cross to Omotesando when you want calmer, upscale browsing.
  • Great area to just walk and watch the style go by.
