黒門市場
Day 11

Kuromon Market

Osaka's kitchen

A 190-year-old covered market where Osaka's chefs shop and visitors graze. Grilled scallops, wagyu skewers, fresh uni and giant strawberries, eaten standing at the stall. Osaka's nickname is 'the nation's kitchen' and this is why.

Things to do

01Grilled seafood

Scallops in the shell, butter-soy squid, whole grilled shrimp, pick the stall with the longest local line.

02Wagyu skewers

A5 beef grilled to order, the single best 1,000 yen of the trip.

03Fresh-cut fruit

Japanese melon and white strawberries, absurd and worth it once.

04Takoyaki warm-up

The octopus balls Osaka invented, first round here, judge every later round against it.

Good to know

  • Go hungry before Den Den Town, it's a 5-minute walk away.
  • Cash for the small stalls.
  • Mornings and lunch are prime, it thins by late afternoon.
