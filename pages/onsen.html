温泉
Day 5

Onsen

Japan's hot springs

Volcanic mineral baths are a national pastime and the classic way Japan relaxes. Perfect for tired legs and jet lag, and once you learn the simple etiquette it's pure bliss.

Things to do

01Wash first

You shower and scrub at a seated station before you ever get in the bath.

02Rotenburo

The outdoor baths, soaking in steaming water with a mountain or sky view.

03Private family bath

Many inns offer a kashikiri, a private bath you can book if you'd rather not share.

04The small towel

You carry a little towel for modesty, but keep it out of the water, resting it on your head.

Good to know

  • Baths are traditionally bare, no swimsuits, and split by gender.
  • Soak 10 to 15 minutes, rinse off, and drink water after.
  • Tattoos can be an issue at some baths, a private bath sidesteps it.
