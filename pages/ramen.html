Day 1

Ramen

Japan's soul food in a bowl

A first bowl after landing is a rite of passage. Every region has its own style and Tokyo has all of them, from a five-dollar counter to bowls people line up an hour for.

Things to do

01Shoyu

Tokyo's home style, a clear soy-based broth. Clean and classic.

02Tonkotsu

Rich, creamy pork-bone broth from Kyushu. The crowd-pleaser.

03Miso

Hearty and a little sweet, made for cold nights, born in Sapporo.

04Tsukemen

Thick noodles served dry with a concentrated dipping broth on the side. Dip and slurp.

05Ichiran

Famous chain where you eat in a private booth and never see the staff. Great first bowl.

Good to know

  • Many shops order through a vending machine at the door, buy a ticket, hand it over.
  • Slurping is encouraged, it cools the noodles and is a compliment.
  • Tokyo Station's Ramen Street lines up eight top shops in one basement.
