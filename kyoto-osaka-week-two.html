Tokyo→Kyoto→Nara→Osaka→Hiroshima
Day5

South on the Bullet Train

Tokyo → Kyoto · Thu Aug 6

Pack up, grab an ekiben on the platform, and do the ride itself — 2 hours 15 to Kyoto, Mt. Fuji out the right-side window. You land mid-day, which is exactly enough for a market lunch and a first walk through the old city.

  • •The Shinkansen south — ekiben in hand, Fuji-side seats reserved↗
  • •Graze lunch through Nishiki Market — Kyoto's 400-year-old kitchen↗
  • •Evening in Gion — lanterns, teahouses, and maybe a geisha slipping past↗
Check in · lanterns after dark
Day6

The Kyoto Postcard Day

Fushimi Inari + Higashiyama · Fri Aug 7

The two most famous sights in Japan, connected by the best old-town walk in the country. Start at sunrise — it beats the August heat and you get the red gates nearly to yourselves.

  • •Fushimi Inari at 7am — ten thousand red gates up the mountain↗
  • •Kiyomizu-dera — the temple on stilts hanging over the city↗
  • •Wander down through Higashiyama — stone lanes, tea, and craft shops into Gion↗
Early start · slow afternoon
Day7

Deer, Buddha & Tea

Nara + Uji · Sat Aug 8

Japan's first capital is 45 minutes away: 1,200 wild deer that bow for crackers, and a 500-ton bronze Buddha in a colossal wooden hall. Uji, the tea capital, is on the train line home.

  • •Nara — feed the bowing deer, then the Great Buddha at Todai-ji↗
  • •Uji on the way back — Byodo-in temple and the best matcha on earth↗
  • •Easy Kyoto evening — dinner in Pontocho alley over the river↗
Bow to a deer — it bows back
Day8

Bamboo, Gold, then Osaka

Arashiyama + Kinkaku-ji · Sun Aug 9

Kyoto's grand finale in one sweep — the bamboo forest early, the Golden Pavilion at midday — then a 15-minute bullet-train hop and you're checked into Osaka by dinner.

  • •Arashiyama at 8am — the bamboo grove, then monkeys with a view over the city↗
  • •Kinkaku-ji — the pavilion wrapped in real gold, floating on its pond↗
  • •Train to Osaka · first neon night on Dotonbori↗
Kyoto out · Osaka in
Day9

The Big One

Super Nintendo World · Mon Aug 10

Walk through a warp pipe and come out inside a life-size Mushroom Kingdom. This is the confirmed yes, so it gets a full day, on a Monday, when the park is at its calmest — which in August still means: go at rope drop.

  • •Super Nintendo World first — Mario Kart in Bowser's Castle, Donkey Kong's coaster↗
  • •Power-Up Band coin battle all day — Dad vs Felix, scores don't lie↗
  • •Collapse over takoyaki back in the city↗
Express Pass on sale now — buy this week
Day10

Osaka, Player Two

Den Den Town + Dotonbori · Tue Aug 11

Osaka's answer to Akihabara — cheaper, less touristy, deeper bargain bins — plus the loudest food street in Japan and an eight-floor Round1 arcade to close the night. Akihabara Day, the sequel.

  • •Graze lunch at Kuromon Market — wagyu skewers and grilled scallops↗
  • •Den Den Town — retro hunting round two, second chance on anything Tokyo↗
  • •Dotonbori at night — the Glico man, okonomiyaki, Round1 till late↗
Arcade + food day · Felix's Osaka
Day11

The Hiroshima Day

Peace Park + Miyajima · Wed Aug 12

Ninety minutes west on the bullet train for the day people most often call the best of their whole trip. The Peace Memorial in the morning, Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki for lunch, and the floating torii of Miyajima in the afternoon.

  • •Hiroshima — the A-Bomb Dome and the Peace Memorial Museum, done properly↗
  • •Ferry to Miyajima — the great torii in the sea, deer in the streets↗
  • •Nozomi back — home in Osaka by bedtime↗
Optional by design — flexes out if you're beat
Day12

Last Morning & Home

Osaka Castle · Thu Aug 13

One calm castle morning in the park, a last market bite, and the ride to the airport. Thirteen days, done right.

  • •Morning walk at Osaka Castle — gold-trimmed tower over 400-year-old walls↗
  • •One last market breakfast and the final souvenir sweep↗
  • •A farewell bowl of ramen before the flight↗
